I am a fourth year PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at Cornell University and a visiting PhD student at the Stanford AI Lab. I am advised by Ashutosh Saxena. Recently I have been working on several sensory rich perception problems with deep learning for cars, humans, and robots. Broadly my thesis is about interactive learning human-robot systems, and my recent projects are Brain4Cars and RoboBrain. At Stanford I also collaborate with Silvio Savarese. I feel fortunate to have collaborated and mentored by Thorsten Joachims, Silvio Savarese, Manik Varma, and S V N Vishwanathan. I graduated from IIT Delhi in 2012 with a Bachelors degree in Electrical Engineering and Masters in Information and Communication Technology.

Research

My research address two important questions in robotics: (i) how can robots learn from sub-optimal human signals? and (ii) how to share learned knowledge across robots? In order to answer these questions I develop machine learning algorithms and build systems that allow users to interact with robots. I have worked on deep learning for sensor rich applications (cars, robots etc.), path planning, and crowsourcing feedback. My research is summarized by the following projects and it has appeared in popular press.
